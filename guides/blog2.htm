Easy Ways to Speed Up Your Computer

Computers slow down over time, which is completely normal after years of constant use. However, you can speed up your computer easily by following these simple & quick tricks!

Why Do Our Computers Slow Down?

There are many factors contributing to why computers slow down over time, such as outdated software, hardware limitations, bloatware, and even viruses.

Without further ado, let's go over these easy tricks to boost your computer's speed!

Stop Heavy Programs

You can manually close programs that you don't use. Alternatively, use the Task Manager to see which programs are consuming the most resources. Right-click the Taskbar and select Task Manager or use the shortcut Ctrl + Shift + Esc.

From there, select the program/application that's consuming the most resources and click End Task.

Optimize Startup Programs

Optimize startup programs to speed up boot time. Open Task Manager, go to the Startup tab, and disable any non-essential applications.

Remove Unwanted Programs

Remove unnecessary programs by searching for "Add or Remove Programs" in the Start menu. You can sort programs by Name, Size, or Install Date, then click Uninstall on the selected program.

You can also use the Control Panel's "Uninstall a Program" option for the same task.

Remove Bloatware

Bloatware consists of unwanted pre-installed software that takes up space and resources. You can remove it manually or use a bloatware removal tool.

For a tool, I recommend using the Windows 10 Debloater by Sycnex, available on GitHub. Download it here.

Ensure you have PowerShell installed, which you can get here. Extract the downloaded file and open the GUI option "Windows10DebloaterGUI" with PowerShell, then click Remove All Bloatware.

Change Your Browser

Switch to a lighter browser that consumes fewer resources than Chrome. Here are some alternatives: Firefox, Opera GX, Pale Moon, Brave.

Purge Unused Extensions!

Disable any unused extensions in your browser as they consume resources. In Chrome, access extensions through the menu (three dots on the top right), then select Manage Extensions.

Toggle off any unnecessary extensions from this menu.

Change Battery Settings

This is for laptops, which often reserve some performance to save battery. To maximize performance, ensure your laptop is plugged in, then click on the Battery Icon in the system tray and adjust the slider to Best Performance.

Upgrade Your Hardware

If these solutions do not improve your computer's performance, consider upgrading your hardware:

  • Upgrade RAM: Helps handle multiple applications efficiently.
  • Upgrade GPU: Boosts performance for gaming and video processing.
  • Upgrade to an SSD: Reduces loading times significantly.
